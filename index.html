Jamie Nam

PhD Researcher in Information Technology · HEC Montréal

I am a first-year PhD student in Information Technology at HEC Montréal. My research integrates digital health systems, business, and user-centered design to improve healthcare accessibility by addressing technology adoption challenges. I am particularly interested in AI-induced identity threats and how the growing capability of these intelligent systems disrupts professionals' sense of expertise, role, and value.

Research Interests

Healthcare Technology Adoption

How people adopt (or not) new digital tools aka IT resistance.

Human–AI Interaction

Decision support systems were meant to augment human judgment in high-stakes settings, but growing reliance on them risks atrophying the very expertise they depend on.

Digital Health Adherence

Wearables and connected devices, and the behavioral patterns that sustain (or end) their use.
